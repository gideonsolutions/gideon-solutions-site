Gideon Solutions

Build systems that hold.

Gideon Solutions is a technical consultancy for organizations that need infrastructure they can trust. We work with institutions, nonprofits, and mission-driven projects that prioritize correctness, transparency, and long-term stability over speed or scale.

What We Do

Technical Architecture Reviews and System Audits

We examine existing systems for structural weaknesses, security concerns, and maintainability problems. You receive a written assessment with clear findings and practical recommendations.

Transparency, Accountability, and Audit Tooling

We design and build systems that support oversight—audit logs, access controls, verification mechanisms, and documentation that makes accountability possible rather than performative.

Open-Source System Design and Advisory

We help organizations adopt, contribute to, and build on open-source infrastructure responsibly. This includes dependency evaluation, contribution strategy, and sustainable integration patterns.

Data Integrity and Governance Systems

We build data pipelines, storage systems, and documentation practices that preserve correctness over time. This includes schema design, validation, lineage tracking, and governance frameworks.

Public-Interest and Institutional Technical Advisory

We advise organizations navigating complex technical decisions—vendor selection, build-vs-buy tradeoffs, compliance requirements, and strategic technology planning—without conflicts of interest.

Websites as Infrastructure

We build and maintain simple, durable, low-maintenance websites for organizations that need security, accessibility, and long-term stability. These are not marketing sites or conversion funnels. They are institutional infrastructure: correct, secure, and designed to remain unchanged for years without attention.

Long-Term Maintenance and Support

We provide ongoing maintenance for systems we build or inherit. This includes security updates, dependency management, documentation upkeep, and careful stewardship of systems that must remain operational.

Who We Work With

  • Churches and Christian organizations
  • Nonprofit organizations and foundations
  • Research institutions and universities
  • Public-interest technology projects
  • Archival and cultural preservation efforts
  • Organizations operating in high-stakes or low-trust environments

Not a Fit

We are not the right partner for growth-stage startups optimizing for speed, advertising or surveillance technology, marketing agencies seeking technical execution, or organizations that need frequent redesigns driven by campaigns or trends. If your primary goal is rapid iteration or conversion optimization, we recommend looking elsewhere.

How We Work

Clear Scope, Written Deliverables

Every engagement has defined boundaries. We produce written documentation—assessments, specifications, runbooks—that remain useful after the engagement ends. You should be able to maintain or hand off any system we build.

Boring, Proven Solutions

We prefer established tools with long track records over new frameworks. We avoid unnecessary complexity. The goal is a system that works correctly and remains understandable to future maintainers.

Correctness Over Speed

We take time to understand problems before proposing solutions. We test assumptions. We document decisions. This approach is slower at the outset but produces systems that hold.

Maintenance Retainers

For organizations that need ongoing support, we offer maintenance arrangements covering security updates, monitoring, and incremental improvements. Terms are negotiated based on system complexity and response requirements.

Why Gideon

The name references Gideon from the Bible—a figure remembered for restraint, not scale. When offered a large army, he reduced it. The lesson: capability is not measured by size, and more is not always better.

This reflects how we approach technical work. We prefer smaller, correct solutions over sprawling systems. We say no to work that isn't a good fit. We believe disciplined decision-making and clear boundaries produce better outcomes than endless expansion.
